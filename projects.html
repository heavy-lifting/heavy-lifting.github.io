--- layout: default title: projects --- Music: Heavy Lifting (2016 - present) - Heavy Lifting is my main solo project, using TidalCycles to scramble samples into seasick beats. For fans of woodwork and minor deities. Studio albums: Thanks for Watching (2019) - songs about weddings, karaoke, getting your head chopped off, the cyclops Polyphemus, bad housemates and lost property. Mortal Springs (2018) - myths. demeter and persephone. a live-coded improvisation using TidalCycles. a language used to describe & generate patterns. a collage of different samples. synth & drum sounds. a small amount of vocals thrown in for good measure. TYPE (2017 - present) - The Yorkshire Programming Ensemble (TYPE) is a collection of humans, Lucy Cheesman, Laurie Johnson, Ryan Kirkbride, and Innocent Granger, that meet IRL and URL and enjoy exploring rhythmic music in a collaborative virtual space and generally making a lot of noise. TRVE YORKSHIRE KVLT ENSEMBLE (2017 - present) - live coded black metal and noise Heavy Bleeding (2017) - a collaboration with now-defunct aggrobeat band Blood Sport. Projects: LANDMASS (2019-2020) - new audiovisual work based on a recent research trip to Orkney. The project combines analogue photography with digital data visualisation and sonification techniques to create an immersive audiovisual installation. SONA examine the living Orcadian landscape from the archaeological remains of the human past through to the future of renewable energy. Sonic Intrigues (2019) - a site-specific multi-channel sound installation exploring metalworking processes through sound at the David Mellor cutlery factory in Hathersage, UK. Re-writing the future (2019) - A series of workshops for families and teenagers at Site Gallery using sound art to respond to the theme of ‘re-writing the future’ Antigone (2019) - A new chorus performance exclusive to Algomech festival includes a piece of music composed using rhythmic structures inspired by the metrical and grammatical structures of Sophocles' original Greek verse. Automation and Me (2019) - a weeklong hackathon and symposium as part of Leeds International Festival exploring the themes of bodies, technologies and automation.
